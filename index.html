Artist Statement

The cause and effect necessary to make art enthralls me.
Ceramics though? This is what engages me.

I imagine my fingers, activated, pressing fingertip finger pressure into the clay, and then knuckle notching a cylinder upward.

Right behind me, heat from a bisque is in mid-fire & only getting hotter. I am wearing overalls.

I see myself as a potter, a prairie potter! In everything I make, ceramic or not, I am making it with a potter's hand.

Urn Series

Tabernacle

2022 · Porcelain, brass, glass, gems and resin · 11" × 13" × 20"

Throughout the ages, man has held sacred the resting place of his departed loved ones. This piece, one in a series of customized Burial Urns, offers a sacred space for the essence of one who has died and honors their unique life and the organic vessel of their soul.

As a maker, my urns must be worthy of what they contain. The decorative form, and its variety of materials — porcelain, brass, glass, gems and resin — are secondary to its utilitarian function: a tabernacle of essence.

Bronze Series

Bronze Bubblegum

2025 · Bronze & Pearls · 35" × 30" × 42"

Bronze Bubblegum is a study in balance: pitting the rigid permanence of bronze against the fleeting, reckless enjoyment of a bubblegum moment.

This sculpture begins as a familiar resin form, a Breyer horse, only to be enthusiastically transformed through a powerful, almost alchemical sequence of applied heat, movement and the reshaping of context.

The final bronze permanently seals this new, edgy narrative — a bronze horse forever blowing a brazen piece of bronze bubblegum!

Curriculum Vitae

Shaping the sacred from earth & fire

Jonathan Bridges is a full-time studio artist working in porcelain, bronze, iron, brass, and mixed media. His practice explores the tension between joy and brutality — creating ornate vessels and visceral sculptural forms that honor both the sacred and the profane.
